# Ignis-Vor: Devourer of Blood and Steel  
*A sentient weapon concept for Kaelen Veylith – Half-Drow Paladin / Hexblade*  
*Campaign: Highmoon, Dalelands*

---

## I. Core Concept

Ignis-Vor is no ordinary blade. It is a **living, parasitic entity** bound to Kaelen’s bloodline – a **devouring weapon of blood and steel** that must feed regularly and hungers to consume other weapons. Every weapon it swallows is added to a grim internal library; Ignis-Vor can then shift its form to perfectly replicate any blade, hammer, or spear it has devoured. For Kaelen this means he can change his weapon without ever sacrificing the dark roleplay bond he shares with the entity. He is a paladin of Tyr struggling to wield a curse, not a cursed man pretending to be a paladin.

---

## II. The Entity: Personality, Alignment & Symbiote Vibe

- **Alignment:** Neutral Evil (DM’s choice between Neutral and Chaotic Evil).  
- **Personality:** Aggressive, manipulative, utterly self-interested. It behaves like a parasitic symbiote – think Venom, but made of shadow and old blood. It whispers, tempts, and pushes Kaelen towards the raw power of the Hexblade path.  
- **Self‑Preservation First:** Ignis-Vor gains nothing from a dead host. If Kaelen perishes, the blade sinks into a cold, powerless dormancy for years, decades, or centuries, waiting for another bearer with the cursed mark to stumble upon it. This single fact shapes everything: the entity will push Kaelen, but never to his outright destruction.  
- **The Two Shoulders:** Kaelen’s life is a constant tug-of-war. Ignis‑Vor is the corrupting whisper on one side, urging indulgence, bloodshed, and swift power. Kaelen’s Paladin training – discipline, oaths, prayer, mental armour – is the opposing angel, the force he uses to hold the entity at bay.

---

## III. The Bloodline Curse: Why the Blade Stays with Kaelen

Ignis‑Vor is not a trinket that can be stolen or thrown away.

- **The Cursed Mark:** The weapon is spiritually anchored to a specific bloodline. Anyone without the mark who tries to lift it finds only an impossibly heavy, inert slab of dead iron – often growing blistering hot or biting cold until they drop it.  
- **The Forced Bond:** Kaelen cannot simply abandon the blade. Because of the mark, Ignis-Vor is tethered to his soul. Leaving it behind does not free him; the starvation penalties worsen, the whispers grow louder and more maddening, and he is compelled to recover it. His only real choice is to master the entity before it masters him.

---

## IV. Cosmic Motivation & The Library of Forms

Ignis-Vor sees the world as a forge of raw materials. When it devours a weapon, it is not merely copying – it is rewriting its own physical reality.

- **The Long Game:** The entity wants Kaelen to hunt powerful foes, find legendary steel, and become a terrifying warrior. A stronger host means access to rarer metals and richer blood.  
- **The Library of Forms:** Every weapon consumed is permanently added to an inner “library.” Ignis-Vor can then assume that exact shape, weight, and balance at will, making Kaelen a walking arsenal – one that grows with every slain enemy and every shattered blade.

---

## V. Family Lineage & DM Campaign Hooks

Kaelen’s parents were killed by bandits in the Dalelands. That event is a closed door, but its consequence is what matters: Kaelen is now the last known bearer of the Cursed Mark.

- **A Bloodstained History:** Past generations handled Ignis-Vor with varying degrees of tragedy. Some ancestors broke completely, becoming bloodthirsty monsters that the Church of Tyr had to hunt down. Others succeeded only through extreme isolation. Kaelen’s path – balancing a holy oath with a dark symbiote – is a tightrope none have walked before.  
- **Hidden Branches:** Kaelen *believes* his immediate line is entirely gone. However, because Ignis-Vor is bound to the bloodline, the DM has complete creative freedom to reveal distant cousins, secret bastards, or faded marks in forgotten relatives – some of whom might very much want the Devouring Blade for themselves.

---

## VI. The Hunger Mechanic

The weapon must feed; if it does not, it begins to take what it needs from Kaelen himself. The following table details the methods available to him.

| Feeding Method | Effect | Downsides / Consequences |
|----------------|--------|--------------------------|
| Defeating a worthy opponent (humanoid / strong creature) | Fully satisfies the hunger. | None. |
| Using his own blood (Self‑Sacrifice) | Resets hunger completely. | Next day begins at 70 % max HP. Suffers roleplay exhaustion and **–1d3** to all ability checks (Persuasion, Insight, Intimidation, etc.) for that day. |
| Stored blood (vials from previous worthy foes) | Delays hunger by 1–2 days. | Blood expires after a few days; not a permanent fix. |
| Prayer & Discipline (Church of Tyr rituals) | Slows hunger progression by 1–2 additional days. | Requires dedicated time; cannot be used as an instant fix. |
| Unwilling sacrifice (drawing blood without killing) | Temporarily resets hunger. | Heavy moral and social consequences – Kaelen will not do this lightly. |

**The self‑sacrifice option** is designed as a last resort. The next day, Kaelen starts with only 70 % of his maximum hit points (rounded down), suffers physical sluggishness and mental fog, and takes a **–1d3 penalty** to all roleplay ability checks. These effects fade after a full day’s rest, or earlier if the DM permits magical restoration.

### Starvation Progression

If Ignis-Vor is not fed at all, pressure builds gradually rather than delivering an instant punishment:

| Hunger Level | Effect on Kaelen |
|--------------|------------------|
| Satisfied | Stable; no ill effects. |
| Mild Hunger (one missed feeding) | Slight restlessness, but no mechanical penalties. |
| Moderate Hunger (two long rests unfed) | The weapon feels heavier; his grip trembles faintly when holding it. |
| Severe Hunger (three long rests unfed) | Whispers invade his thoughts, dreams turn dark; loses **1d6 HP** upon waking. |
| Starving (four or more long rests unfed) | Brief blackouts occur. When awareness returns, the blade is already in his hand – dripping fresh blood. |

This graduated system creates rising tension and meaningful choice, rather than forcing combat every few days.

## VII. Metamorphosis: Devouring Weapons, Taking Forms

Ignis-Vor’s defining feature is its ability to **consume other weapons and adopt their shape**. This replaces the standard flexibility of the Pact of the Blade feature, tying his arsenal directly to what he has fed the blade.

- **Absorption:** As an action, Kaelen can touch a non‑magical weapon and let Ignis-Vor consume it. The weapon is destroyed, and the sentient blade permanently adds that weapon’s form to its library. Magical weapons may require a successful ritual, a quest, or DM adjudication – absorbing one might only grant the physical shape without replicating its enchantments, but this is ment for weapons which is freely given by the DM, so I am not gaining anything from eating a boss monsters weapon and expect to have that power. It allows a workaround the "hexblade can only use one weapon" feature as well and that can open up for the +1 blade or other types of weapons.
- **Shifting Form:** As a bonus action, Kaelen can command Ignis-Vor to reshape itself into any weapon stored in its library. The new form behaves exactly like his pact weapon and maintains all class features (Hex Warrior, invocations, etc.). He may switch as often as he likes, but only between forms he has “fed” to the entity.

This means Kaelen can walk into a fight with a longsword, devour a fallen enemy’s warhammer, and moments later bring it down as a maul – all without ever breaking the dark narrative bond with his blade.

---

## VIII. Why This Is Balanced & Fun

✅ **Scales With Level:** Self‑sacrifice uses a percentage of HP, so the cost remains meaningful from 1st to 20th level.  
✅ **Rich Roleplay Choices:** Kaelen can manage hunger through combat, prayer, preserved blood, or costly self‑harm – each choice says something about his character.  
✅ **Pressure, Not Punishment:** The hunger builds over several days; it never forces the player into an unwanted fight.  
✅ **Encourages Creative Problem‑Solving:** A non‑combat heavy campaign works perfectly because vials, rituals, and careful planning are just as viable as bloodshed.  
✅ **Weapon Evolution Through Play:** Every absorbed weapon expands Kaelen’s options, rewarding exploration and combat milestones with tangible narrative growth.  
✅ **Inherent Roleplay Tension:** The angel‑and‑devil dynamic between Tyr’s discipline and the symbiote’s whispers keeps every decision charged.
